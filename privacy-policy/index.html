CC Wallet Privacy Policy

Last updated: October 20, 2024

Dear users,

                CC Wallet is committed to safeguarding the privacy, security, and safety of its users. Our privacy policy is grounded in the principle of consent, ensuring that users are informed and in control of their personal information. Below are key elements of our privacy policy that highlight our dedication to protecting user data.

1. Interpretation and Definitions

  • Interpretation: The words of which the initial letter is capitalized have meanings defined under the following conditions. The following definitions shall have the same meaning regardless of whether they appear in singular or in plural.
  • Definitions: For the purposes of this Privacy Policy:
  • Account: means a unique account created for you to access our service or parts of our service.
  • Application: refers to Core Custodian – CC Wallet, the software program provided by the Core Custodian – CC Wallet.
  • Company: (referred to as either “the Company”, “We”, “Us” or “Our” in this Agreement) refers to Core Custodian – CC Wallet.
  • Device: means any device that can access the service such as a computer, a cellphone or a digital tablet.
  • Personal Data: is any information that relates to an identified or identifiable individual.
  • Service refers: to the application.
  • Service Provider: means any natural or legal person who processes the data on behalf of the Core Custodian – CC Wallet. It refers to third-party companies or individuals employed by the Core Custodian – CC Wallet to facilitate the service, to provide the service on behalf of the Core Custodian – CC Wallet, to perform services related to the Service or to assist the Core Custodian – CC Wallet in analyzing how the Service is used.
  • Usage Data: refers to data collected automatically, either generated by the use of the Service or from the service infrastructure itself (for example, the duration of a page visit).
  • You: means the individual accessing or using the service, or other legal entity on behalf of which such individual is accessing or using the service, as applicable.

2. Collecting and Using Your Personal Data

  • No Collection of Private Key or Passphrase

One of the cornerstone principles of CC Wallet is the non-collection of private keys or passphrases. Users retain full ownership of their keys, ensuring that their crypto assets are secure and under their control at all times. This policy not only enhances security but also minimizes risks associated with potential data breaches, as there are no sensitive private keys stored on our servers.

  • No Sale of Personal Information

CC Wallet operates on the ethos of transparency and trust. We do not sell personal information to any third parties. This commitment ensures that users’ data remains confidential and is not exploited for profit. Our focus is solely on enhancing user experience without compromising personal privacy.

  • Necessary Information Collection

We prioritize minimal data collection. CC Wallet only gathers personal information when it is essential for delivering services or enhancing user experience. This approach ensures that users are not burdened with unnecessary data requests, maintaining a streamlined and efficient service.

  • No Financial Information Collection

While CC Wallet provides users with features such as entry or exit from cryptocurrencies, it does not collect any financial payment or banking information. Users may need to provide this information to third-party providers during transactions, but rest assured, CC Wallet does not store or process this data directly, further reinforcing our commitment to user privacy.

  • IP Address Processing

In order to maintain the security of our platform, CC Wallet may temporarily process IP addresses for specific services. For instance, this is crucial for preventing Distributed Denial-of-Service (DDoS) attacks, which can disrupt service availability. However, this data is used solely for security purposes and is not retained longer than necessary.

  • Wallet Data Privacy

User wallet data is kept private by default. CC Wallet only shares this information with explicit user consent. This means that users have full control over who sees their wallet address, ensuring that their financial activities remain confidential unless they choose to disclose them.

  • Encryption and Data Security

Security is a fundamental aspect of CC Wallet. We employ client-side encryption, meaning that user information is encrypted locally on their devices. The server functions as a secure storage solution, relaying only encrypted data. This architecture minimizes exposure to potential breaches, as sensitive data is never transmitted or stored in an unencrypted format.

  • User Control and Customization

CC Wallet places a strong emphasis on user autonomy. We empower our users to customize and configure their wallets according to their personal needs. This level of control enhances the user experience, allowing individuals to tailor their wallets to fit their preferences and security requirements.

In summary, CC Wallet is dedicated to protecting user privacy while providing a secure and user-friendly experience. By prioritizing user control, minimizing data collection, and implementing stringent security measures, we strive to create a trustworthy environment for all users.

By using our services, you confirm that you are not accessing the platform from within the EU or EEA.

Our Services does not assume any responsibility or liability for data collection, content, or practices of third-party websites. Your interactions with these third parties are solely between you and them.
